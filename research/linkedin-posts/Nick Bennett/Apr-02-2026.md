## Content

7 posts in March. 90,234 impressions. 3.24% avg engagement rate.

No content calendar. No pre-written posts. No batching on Sundays.

Here's the format breakdown:
17 image posts. 61,064 impressions. 3,592 avg per post. 
14 text posts. 24,729 impressions. 3.88% avg engagement rate. 
3 video posts. 4,361 impressions. 2.54% avg engagement rate.

Thanks Shield and andreas jonsson for the data.

I didn't plan any of that split. It just happened.

Most days I opened LinkedIn and thought about what happened today that was worth sharing.

Sometimes it was a client call that surfaced something I hadn't thought about before. 
Sometimes it was a problem I was actively solving. 
Sometimes it was baseball.

That's the whole system.

The data does tell me something useful though.

Images got me in front of more people. Text made more of them actually stop and respond. 23.57 avg comments per text post vs 21.71 for image. Sounds small. Over 37 posts it adds up.

But the numbers I care most about aren't impressions or reactions.

43 saves. 55 profile visits driven directly by posts. 20-30 inbound DMs from people who never comment publicly.

That's the quiet side of LinkedIn nobody talks about.

The baseball post got 84 profile clicks. More than anything else I published all month. It was a text post. About coming back from ACL surgery. Zero marketing content.

Saves mean someone found it useful enough to come back to. Profile visits mean someone got curious about who's behind it. DMs mean someone felt something but didn't want to say it out loud.

Those three things convert. Reactions don't.

Reach and resonance are different goals. Sometimes the same post hits both. Most of the time you're picking one.

I'm not saying planning is bad. Plenty of people build great content that way.

But something gets lost when you script everything in advance. The posts that hit hardest are written within an hour of the thing actually happening.

Realness has a texture. Readers can feel the difference between I just lived this and I wrote this on Sunday for Thursday.

I post when I have something worth saying. Some days that's once. Some days that's twice. Some days nothing.

No algorithm optimization. No best time to post spreadsheet. I just know that when I share something real, someone out there needs to hear it.

That's enough of a reason.
