## Content

How do you measure organic social?

How do you think about the ROI of social media?

Here's how I think about it, but tell me if I'm wrong:

People tell us - if you know, you know. There are micro signs.

When you start writing on LinkedIn over time (assuming you're writing useful, valuable, specific, interesting content) you will start to get more engagement. More engagement = likes, comments, DMs. All of a sudden, someone DMs you to say they liked what you wrote for the first time ... ever. Then someone invites you on a podcast. Then a key partner tells you they saw that thing you wrote on LinkedIn... this is how it happens.

I DM every new member that joins Exit Five and they will tell me proactively how they heard about Exit Five.

We also ask every new member during onboarding how they heard about us (self reported attribution).

We also can look at traffic to the website from social, and I also use direct traffic as a proxy here. The more people who know about us from social media = more people will know about us, and when more people know about you direct traffic to your website grows.

How do you think about measuring the ROI of social media for your business?

What would you add here?
