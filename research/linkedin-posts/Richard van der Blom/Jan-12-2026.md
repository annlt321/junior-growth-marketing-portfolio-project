## Content

8 predictions about LinkedIn in 2026. 
Half of LinkedIn experts will pretend this post doesn’t exist.

I've watched LinkedIn evolve for almost 20 years.
First for the better. Now? I'm not so sure.

**𝟭. 𝗠𝗼𝘀𝘁 𝗟𝗶𝗻𝗸𝗲𝗱𝗜𝗻 𝘁𝗿𝗮𝗶𝗻𝗲𝗿𝘀 𝘄𝗶𝗹𝗹 𝗯𝗲 𝗼𝘂𝘁 𝗼𝗳 𝗮 𝗷𝗼𝗯.**
Functional training dies when AI and AI agents can teach the basics faster than any course. The ones who survive? Those who bring strategy and direction, not just frameworks and templates. 

**𝟮. 𝗣𝗲𝗼𝗽𝗹𝗲 𝘄𝗶𝗹𝗹 𝗴𝗲𝘁 𝗳𝗲𝗱 𝘂𝗽 𝘄𝗶𝘁𝗵 𝗔𝗜 𝗽𝗼𝘀𝘁𝘀, 𝗰𝗼𝗺𝗺𝗲𝗻𝘁𝘀 𝗮𝗻𝗱 𝗮𝘂𝘁𝗼𝗺𝗮𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻.**
Your AI twin booking calls? That bubble will burst. I can spot AI comments in under a minute. So can your prospects. The "scale everything" crowd will learn that automation without authenticity destroys trust faster than it builds pipeline. 

**𝟯. 𝗬𝗼𝘂𝗿 𝗮𝘂𝗱𝗶𝗲𝗻𝗰𝗲 𝗶𝘀𝗻'𝘁 𝗼𝘄𝗻𝗲𝗱. 𝗜𝘁'𝘀 𝗲𝗮𝗿𝗻𝗲𝗱.**
Stop engaging your most interesting followers? Your ambassadors will swap you for someone else within weeks. Attention isn't loyalty. The algorithm doesn't owe you anything. Neither does your audience. 

**𝟰. 𝗧𝗵𝗲 "𝗜 𝘄𝗮𝘀 𝗯𝗿𝗼𝗸𝗲, 𝗻𝗼𝘄 𝗜 𝗺𝗮𝗸𝗲 𝟲 𝗳𝗶𝗴𝘂𝗿𝗲𝘀" 𝗰𝗿𝗼𝘄𝗱 𝘄𝗶𝗹𝗹 𝗴𝗲𝘁 𝗲𝘅𝗽𝗼𝘀𝗲𝗱.**
Monthly income screenshots. Rags-to-riches stories. Addiction-to-success narratives. People are getting smarter. The BS detector is sharpening. 

**𝟱.𝗟𝗲𝘀𝘀 𝗺𝗮𝗶𝗻𝘀𝘁𝗿𝗲𝗮𝗺. 𝗠𝗼𝗿𝗲 𝗻𝗶𝗰𝗵𝗲.**
Mainstream content slowly dies with AI newsletters like 360Brew. Settle for smaller but more engaged audiences. Stay in your lane instead of being a jack of all trades. Generic advice gets buried. Specific expertise gets rewarded. The algorithm is learning to match depth with intent.

**𝟲. 𝗦𝗼𝗰𝗶𝗮𝗹 𝗽𝗿𝗼𝗼𝗳 𝗹𝗶𝗲𝘀 𝗶𝗻 𝗾𝘂𝗮𝗹𝗶𝘁𝘆, 𝗻𝗼𝘁 𝗾𝘂𝗮𝗻𝘁𝗶𝘁𝘆.**
500k followers with 1,000 "Great post!" comments at the same time? We see through it. The conversations in your comments need to show depth and added value. Buyers now scroll the comments before they scroll your profile. Empty engagement is a red flag, not a badge of honor.

**𝟳. 𝗟𝗶𝗻𝗸𝗲𝗱𝗜𝗻 𝘄𝗶𝗹𝗹 𝗹𝗼𝘀𝗲 𝗶𝘁𝘀 𝗯𝗶𝗴𝗴𝗲𝘀𝘁 𝗰𝗿𝗲𝗮𝘁𝗼𝗿𝘀.**
The ones actually making money are building email lists and growing on other platforms. Treating LinkedIn as a funnel, not a home. The platform will notice too late. When your reach depends on an algorithm you don't control, smart creators diversify. LinkedIn becomes the top of funnel, not the whole funnel.

**𝟴. 𝗠𝗼𝗿𝗲 𝗽𝗲𝗼𝗽𝗹𝗲 𝘄𝗶𝗹𝗹 𝘀𝘁𝗮𝗿𝘁 𝗰𝗼𝗵𝗼𝗿𝘁𝘀 𝗯𝗲𝗰𝗮𝘂𝘀𝗲 𝘁𝗵𝗲𝘆 𝗷𝗼𝗶𝗻𝗲𝗱 𝗼𝗻𝗲.**
People who didn't post 12 months ago are now selling "complete cohort structures." They received a roadmap and now they're teaching it. Will you see through it? Check their track record before you buy. Experience can't be downloaded from a Notion template.

I help people grow on LinkedIn for a living.
And I'm telling you: half this industry won't exist in 12 months.
